# Soy & Mochi — Character Style Rules

These are the confirmed style standards for this project, refined through the first generation session. Always apply these in every generation prompt.

---

## Outlines

- **Body/fur/clothing/ears/tail outlines** → dark maroon (warm dark reddish-brown), matching the 360-degree turn reference sheets. NOT pure black.
- **Eye lines, nose lines, mouth lines** → pure solid BLACK only. This creates a visual contrast that makes the faces pop.

---

## Mochi (hamster) — specific rules

- **Nose shape** → small downward-pointing triangle (like a cat nose). NOT a round dot.
- **Cheek markings** → two small pink swirl/spiral lines on each cheek, exactly as shown on the Mochi 360-turn reference sheet. Always present.
- **Cheeks** → naturally round and chubby. When eating or holding food, make them slightly puffier.

---

## Soy (fox) — specific rules

- **Tail** → fluffy, warm orange-red. Always fully visible when possible.
- **Heart marking** → small heart on chest, always present.

---

## Background & Scene

- Background colors should be warm but NOT overpowering. Soft golden/pastel tones preferred. Avoid backgrounds that are too saturated or dominant — the characters should always be the focal point.

---

## What to always reference

When generating any scene with both characters:
- Always use `--max-refs 3`: both 360-turn sheets + the latest approved `together/` reference
- Keep prompts simple on character description — the turn sheets do the visual anchoring
- For corrections: promote the approved image to `refs/together/`, then anchor to it with "reproduce EXACTLY, change only X"
